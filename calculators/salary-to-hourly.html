Understanding Your True Earnings

While an annual salary gives you a big-picture view of your income, converting it to an hourly rate helps you understand the value of your time. This is especially useful when comparing job offers that have different work-hour requirements or when considering overtime potential.

Usage Example: $65,000 Salary Breakdown

If you earn a $65,000 annual salary and work a standard 40-hour week for 52 weeks a year, your hourly rate is $31.25. If you take 2 weeks of unpaid leave (working 50 weeks), your hourly rate for those worked hours would be $32.50. This tool helps you see how much each hour of your life is valued by your employer.

Salary FAQ

How is the hourly rate calculated? +
The most common method is dividing the annual salary by the total number of work hours in a year (typically 2,080 hours for a 40-hour work week and 52 weeks).
Does this include taxes? +
This calculator shows "gross" income (before taxes). Your "take-home" pay will be lower after federal, state/local taxes, and insurance deductions.
